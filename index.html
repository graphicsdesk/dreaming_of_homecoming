DREAMING OF HOMECOMING

Painting My Return to New York City


By Laila Cruz

Edited by Helen Yang

As a lucid dreamer, I control every aspect of my nightly explorations with an unexplainable awareness. There are two rules to my dreams: I cannot see what I have never seen, and I cannot feel what I have never felt. Every night for the past couple of weeks, I have visited only one place: New York City.

I spent my childhood climbing boulders in Central Park, eating a poppy seed bagel on the way to school, and falling asleep to the white noise of the West Side Highway. The city was my home and my sanctuary. And now, as an incoming student, it is my future.

To prepare for my return, I revisited my memories through childhood photos and documented my dreams in paintings. I fear that because of the COVID-19 pandemic, the city will not live up to my expectations. What is New York City without the bustling streets, crowded subways, and myriad of diverse faces?

But in painting my dreams, I realized that they are only a sketch of what the future could look like. New York City is a blank canvas, and we each hold a brush.
